# Configuring Custom Domains in Vercel\n\nThis document outlines the steps to configure custom domains for your project deployed on Vercel.\n\n## Prerequisites\n\n*   Your project is deployed on [Vercel](https://vercel.com/).\n*   You have access to your domain provider\'s DNS settings.\n\n## Steps\n\n1.  **Add Your Domain to Your Vercel Project:**\n    a.  Go to your project\'s dashboard on Vercel.\n    b.  Navigate to the **Settings** tab, then select **Domains**.\n    c.  Enter your custom domain (e.g., `www.yourdomain.com` or `yourdomain.com`) in the input field and click **Add**.\n\n2.  **Configure DNS Records:**\n    Vercel will provide you with the necessary DNS records to configure with your domain provider. This usually involves one of two options:\n\n    *   **Option A: Using Nameservers (Recommended for new domains or full domain control)**\n        a.  Vercel will give you a list of Vercel Nameservers (e.g., `ns1.vercel-dns.com`, `ns2.vercel-dns.com`).\n        b.  Log in to your domain provider\'s control panel (e.g., GoDaddy, Namecheap, Cloudflare).\n        c.  Find the section for managing Nameservers and replace your current nameservers with the ones provided by Vercel.\n        d.  **Note:** DNS changes can take up to 48 hours to propagate globally.\n\n    *   **Option B: Using A Record / CNAME Record (For subdomains or if you want to keep your existing DNS provider)**\n        a.  If you\'re adding a root domain (e.g., `yourdomain.com`), Vercel will typically provide an **A record** pointing to Vercel\'s IP address.\n        b.  If you\'re adding a subdomain (e.g., `www.yourdomain.com` or `blog.yourdomain.com`), Vercel will typically provide a **CNAME record** pointing to your Vercel deployment URL (e.g., `cname.vercel-dns.com`).\n        c.  Log in to your domain provider\'s control panel.\n        d.  Navigate to the DNS management section.\n        e.  Add a new record (A or CNAME) as specified by Vercel.\n        f.  **Important:** If adding a root domain with an A record, ensure no other A records for `@` (your root domain) conflict.\n\n3.  **Verify Domain Status in Vercel:**\n    a.  Return to the **Domains** section of your Vercel project.\n    b.  Vercel will automatically detect the DNS changes. Once successfully configured, the status next to your domain will change to **Valid** or **Configured**.\n\n## Troubleshooting\n\n*   **DNS Propagation:** If your domain isn\'t resolving after a few hours, wait a bit longer. DNS changes take time.\n*   **Incorrect DNS Records:** Double-check that you\'ve entered the exact records provided by Vercel into your domain provider\'s settings.\n*   **Existing Records:** Ensure there are no conflicting DNS records (especially A records for the root domain or CNAMEs for subdomains).
